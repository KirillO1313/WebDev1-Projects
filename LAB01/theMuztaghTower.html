The Muztagh Tower

Location

Muztagh Tower (Urdu: مز تاغ ٹاور), also Mustagh Tower; Muztagh: icy mountain, is a mountain situated in Baltoro Muztagh, which is a segment of the Karakoram range. It straddles the border of the Gilgit Baltistan region of Pakistan and the Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region of China. Muztagh Tower is located between the basins of the Baltoro and Sarpo Laggo glaciers.

Rise in Popularity

The Mustagh Tower gained attention through a photograph captured by Vittorio Sella during the 1909 Italian expedition to K2. The picture was taken from the upper Baltoro, from a vantage point located due southeast of the mountain. In this photograph, the twin summits of Mustagh Tower appeared perfectly aligned, giving the mountain the appearance of a slender tooth and making it seem extremely impregnable. This image was featured in a book on mountaineering in 1941 with the caption "The Last Citadel"

Assents

Nearly fifty years after Vittorio Sella’s iconic photograph of the Muztagh Tower, it inspired two expeditions in 1956 to attempt the first ascent. Despite the dramatic appearance in the photo, both teams found the mountain's routes to be less steep than expected. The British team, approaching from the west, summited first on July 6 via the Northwest Ridge, followed five days later by a French team climbing from the east. Several notable ascents followed: a British team completed the second Northwest Ridge ascent in 1984; Göran Kropp and Rafael Jensen made the fourth ascent in 1990; in 2008, Slovenians Pavle Kozjek and Dejan Miškovič climbed the Northeast Face but turned back due to strong winds, with Kozjek tragically falling during descent; and in 2012, a Russian team established a new route via the center of the Northeast Face after a 17-day climb.
